## Overview

Notifications let you leave a Gemini tab while a response is generating. When a standard Gemini reply finishes in a background tab, Gemini Power Kit sends a system notification with the chat title and a short response preview.

Click the notification to return to the conversation that created it. The notification is then cleared from the notification center.

## Why It Matters

Long replies should not force you to keep watching one tab. Notifications help when you:

- work across several Gemini conversations
- switch to another app while waiting for a response
- run a longer prompt and want to continue as soon as it finishes
- need an audible cue instead of repeatedly checking the tab

## Quick Workflow

1. Open the Gemini Power Kit sidebar.
2. Go to **Notifications**.
3. Turn on **Notify when Gemini finishes replying** and approve the requested browser permissions.
4. Optionally turn on **Play a sound with notifications**.
5. Click **Send test notification** to verify your setup.
6. Start a Gemini reply, then switch to another tab or app.
7. Click the completion notification to return to the correct conversation.

## Notification Behavior

- Notifications are sent only when the Gemini page is hidden or does not have focus.
- The alert uses the current chat title and a short preview when they are available.
- If response details cannot be read, you still receive a generic completion alert.
- Clicking an alert focuses its Gemini conversation and clears the alert.
- The extension processes notification content locally and does not read the streaming response body.

## Current Scope

Notifications currently detect standard Gemini replies. Deep Research, Canvas, and other Gemini workflows that do not use the standard reply flow may not create a completion notification.

## Troubleshooting

If the test notification does not appear:

1. Confirm the main notification switch is still enabled.
2. Open [Notification Troubleshooting](/support/notification-troubleshooting/).
3. Allow notifications for your browser in operating-system settings.
4. Check Focus, Do Not Disturb, or other system modes that may hide alerts.
5. Send another test notification.
